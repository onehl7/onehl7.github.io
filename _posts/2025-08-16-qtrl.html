Quantum Computing's Journey

An Interactive Look at Technology Readiness Levels (TRL)

What is Quantum TRL?

Technology Readiness Levels (TRLs) measure a technology's maturity. For quantum computing, a specialized QTRL scale is used. Below are the definitions for each level.

Quantum Technology Readiness Levels (QTRL)

QTRL Level Description
QTRL 1 Theoretical framework for quantum computing is formulated. Basic properties are studied, moving towards applied research.
QTRL 2 Basic device principles are studied, and applications or algorithms are formulated. Technology is speculative with little experimental support.
QTRL 3 Fabricated imperfect physical qubits (basic building blocks) exist. Laboratory studies validate theoretical predictions.
QTRL 4 Multi-qubit systems are fabricated, and classical devices for qubit manipulation are developed and tested together.
QTRL 5 Components are integrated into a small quantum processor without error correction. Rigorous testing is conducted.
QTRL 6 Components are integrated into a small quantum processor with error correction. Rigorous testing is conducted.
QTRL 7 A prototype quantum computer solves small but user-relevant problems and is demonstrated in a user environment.
QTRL 8 A scalable version of a quantum computer is completed and qualified through test and demonstration.
QTRL 9 Quantum computers exceed the computational power of classical computers for general or specific problems.
