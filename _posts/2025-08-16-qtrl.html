Technology Readiness Levels (TRLs) measure a technology's maturity. For quantum computing, a specialized QTRL scale is used. Below are the definitions for each level.

Quantum Technology Readiness Levels (QTRL)

QTRL Level Description
QTRL 1Theoretical framework for quantum computing is formulated.
QTRL 2Basic device principles are studied, and applications or algorithms are formulated.
QTRL 3Fabricated imperfect physical qubits exist. Laboratory studies validate theoretical predictions.
QTRL 4Multi-qubit systems are fabricated, and classical devices for qubit manipulation are developed.
QTRL 5Components are integrated into a small quantum processor without error correction.
QTRL 6Components are integrated into a small quantum processor with error correction.
QTRL 7A prototype quantum computer solves small but user-relevant problems.
QTRL 8A scalable version of a quantum computer is completed and qualified.
QTRL 9Quantum computers exceed the computational power of classical computers for specific problems.
